Events

Vanmahotsav
This program is launched by glendale to give the message of planting more and more plants together.

'Go Green' Initiative - the philosophy of the school is well evident during Vanamahotsav celebrations. Activities include bamboo saplings plantation, students and the staff come dressed in green and there is a Fancy Dress competition, Card making and skits for the younger children. For the seniors, there is a lot of fun and ordered chaos with salad making, Poster Making, Collage Making, Ikebana, Body Art and Photography.

Grandparents Day

Grandparents are our heritage and are the most essential members of our family and community. We celebrate this day to honour our grandparents and to express our gratitude for being their children’s children. The children entertain their grandparents with different performances and a number of games are played to enhance family bonding.

Night Out

The night camp at Glendale is a two day adventure camp comprising activities like rappelling, flying fox, burma rope, zorbing ball, archery, tent pitching and climbing the commando net. The highlights of the camp are treasure hunt and bonfire.

Satran - Art Festival

Drawing inspiration from the highly popular movie 'Taare Zameen Par', Glendale Academy International has been organizing an annual arts festival 'Satrang' since 2007. The event has had a terrific response from the students parents, staff and alumni and their creative expression has superbly authenticated the films tagline, 'Every Child is Special'.

Dear Program

DEAR - DROP EVERTHING AND READ is an endeavour to develop reading skills. The entire school from management staff, students to support staff take part in this initiative. They drop everything and read for 30 minutes.

Farewell

A memorable evening planned for the passing out Grade X students by their juniors as they gear up for new endeavours in the outside world.



What's New

  • STUDYING from home

    As the world is under the blanket of the recent virus we can't attend physical school but the government decided that there should be no impact on school curriculum. Hence schools were notified to run online classes during ongoing pandemic.

    At Glendale we ensure that all the students have fun time along with academic classes during online sessions. We have ensured optimal screen time and a balanced curriculum with a healthy mix of screen time, breaks, activities and assessments to keep our students engaged and the learning outcome optimal!

    To leave no stone unturned our team worked through the summer vacation to upgrade their skills through webinars and training sessions! We created an online encyclopaedia of virtual terms titled * Glendale Online Learning Encyclopedia* and we have also come up with an ABCs of Virtual Learning to guide our teachers in the New Normal.

  • Student Led Coffee Curriculum(SLCC)

    “Student Led Coffee Curriculum” is an initiative which Glendale has been running, in which students during their last term, take the parents and students of next session through the curriculum which they had covered in a year.
    Students give an insight on:

    • Academic Learnings
    • Reflection of Habits
    • Leadership roles
    • Celebrations/Achievements

    As part of this program, students talk about their mission statements, goals, fun activities for academic learnings, celebrations and how they achieved their missions and goals during the year.
